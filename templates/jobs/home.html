Know Before You Apply

Simple, honest advice every Nepali job seeker should know — it can save you money and heartbreak

{% icon 'alert-triangle' size=22 %}

Never Pay for a Job

No legitimate employer in Nepal asks for money to hire you. If someone demands a "processing fee", "training deposit" or "file charge" — walk away. It's a scam, every time.

{% icon 'badge-check' size=22 %}

Going Abroad? Check the License

Before signing with any manpower agency, verify its license on dofe.gov.np. Get your labour permit, keep your own passport, and remember — for many destinations the "Free Visa, Free Ticket" policy applies.

{% icon 'book-open' size=22 %}

Loksewa Has One Official Source

All Lok Sewa Aayog vacancies and results are published at psc.gov.np. Applications are online through the PSC portal. Anyone selling "guaranteed passes" or inside contacts is lying.

{% icon 'graduation-cap' size=22 %}

Real Scholarships Are Free

Government and embassy scholarships never charge application fees. Verify announcements on the ministry, embassy, or university's own website before sharing documents with anyone.

{% icon 'file-check' size=22 %}

Keep Your Documents Yours

Never hand over your original citizenship, passport, or certificates to brokers or "agents". Submit copies; originals only at official offices, and always get a receipt.

{% icon 'search-check' size=22 %}

Verify Before You Trust

Every NepHub listing links to its official source — always click through and confirm details there before applying. If a deadline or salary looks too good to be true, double-check it.
